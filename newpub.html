The Economics

Why this is happening now.

The pub's business model is broken. The replacement's business model is far superior. This isn't a cultural shift — it's an economic inevitability.

£30–40
Per head for a 1-hour splatter paint session. A pub charges £6 for a pint someone nurses for two hours.
Zero
Inventory waste. Paint doesn't spoil. Axes don't go off. Canvases don't need refrigerating. The pub's fresh kegs do.
Class E
Railway arch leases — lower per sq ft than high-street retail. These aren't premium venues. They're better businesses in cheaper buildings.

The modern Third Place doesn't replace the pub's revenue stream — it adds a high-margin activity on top of it. These venues still sell craft beer. People drink while they throw axes or play board games. But the drink is the bonus, not the economic driver.
